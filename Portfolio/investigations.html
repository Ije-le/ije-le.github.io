Chinese Illegal miners deprive government of revenue, exploit child labour in Nasarawa

ICIR · 2024

In Nasarawa State, Nigeria, illegal miners are extracting lithium and selling it. The sales are often traced to Chinese companies, and are carried out without official regulation, depriving the Nigerian government of revenue and undermining a 2022 ban on unprocessed mineral exports. The report also documents the widespread use of child labour, with children as young as 9 or 10 working in dangerous mining conditions, highlighting weak regulatory enforcement and the risk the country may not fully benefit from its mineral resources.

Health and haircuts: College Park resident spreads medical awareness through barbershops

College Park Here & Now · 2025

A College Park resident and public health professor launched a program training barbers and stylists to share accurate health information and host services like vaccine clinics in barbershops, using these trusted community spaces to improve medical awareness and equity.

LGBTQ+: walking the tightrope of social exclusion in Nigeria

ICIR · 2024

In Nigeria, members of the LGBTQ+ community face deep social exclusion, discrimination, family rejection, violence, and limited access to healthcare, forcing many to conceal their identities or turn to unsafe coping mechanisms in a largely homophobic society.

Female councilmembers reflect on political journey

College Park Here & Now · 2025

Female members of the College Park City Council reflect on their personal political journeys, noting initial challenges in a male-dominated field and emphasizing the importance of increasing female representation, diverse perspectives, and community support in local government.
