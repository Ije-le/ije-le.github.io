Background information and design process

This animation video was designed as an explainer for a previously published piece of journalism.

The story, How Abuja community paid nearly N10m to access good roads in three months, showed how government neglect led residents of Kubwa, a community in the Bwari Area Council of Nigeria’s Federal Capital Territory, to collectively fund the construction of their own roads, despite paying taxes.

The aim of the video was to summarize the story, focusing on how a seemingly negligible sum of fifty naira, demanded by a group of women fixing potholes along Liberty Junction, snowballed into a significant amount paid by citizens as a result of government negligence.

To begin the process, I first created a script for the video.

Next, I created a storyboard in which I paired possible clips/images with different sections of the script.

Since I was aiming for a fully animated video, I began the animation process by designing my assets. Most of the assets were hand-drawn in Photoshop and imported into AfterEffects for animation. While at this, I also generated the voice-over for the script on Adobe Premier Pro, which I imported into After Effects during animation.

Some techniques I used repeatedly in putting the video together are position and expressions, and after the animation was done, I exported the video from AfterEffects, imported it to Premier Pro where I generated burned in captions.
