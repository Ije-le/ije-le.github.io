Short Minute Weekly News Recapp

Creating a short video recap of important news stories from the past few days is an effective way to reach a broader audience, as it delivers essential information quickly and clearly. This method of delivering news is for people with fast-paced, busy schedules, short attention spans, and limited free time who may not have the time to sit down and read a full article or watch morning news channels.

Social media platforms like TikTok and Instagram Reels thrive because they can produce short, yet engaging, videos that capture viewers' attention for a brief period. Therefore, these platforms are ideal for news outlets to take advantage of, as they can extend their reach beyond just broadcast TV news and online websites. Gen Z and Millennials are also already accustomed to using these platforms as a source of entertainment, so adapting to the younger generations allows for a larger audience to be reached. Younger audiences will have the opportunity to stay informed and engage with news.

Understanding that the idea is to deliver multiple news stories in under 90 seconds, it's no surprise that these stories might not be as in-depth as every single detail can be read in the full article online. However, the point of these recaps isn't to provide the whole story; it's supposed to give a quick summary of a story. If a viewer is interested in a story relayed through the recap, they can proceed at their own time to view the full story on the news website.

Creating Task

Newsrooms must use regular AI and AI audio-generation platforms to create these auto-generated news recap videos. As an overview, this task can be broken into two sections: generating a script and using an AI Audio generator to make the voice dictate the script. Depending on the platform they choose to release the script on, the newsroom can add pictures for story context if they choose.

Task Part 1: Script Generating

First, the newsroom needs to select which news articles they deem most important for the public from the past week. Since time is short, the newsroom should choose only six stories at most to ensure that the audio has enough time to summarize the story adequately.

Second, the newsroom must implement these stories into the generic AI of their choice, with a basic direction prompt that asks the AI to create a script. The prompt should be clear and specific, suggesting the script features a professional or formal dialogue. It should also be neither too long nor too short, but just enough to fill 60-90 seconds. Virtual Assistance Feedback and Suggestions

This would be a voice assistant like Alexa or Siri that can provide specific feedback and assistance based on what the user asks. The voice assistant can be triggered by text. With voice assistance, the AI would take the question the user had previously requested and provide pathways to related questions that the user might want to ask as a follow-up. The AI is mainly used as a predictive language, as it should be able to anticipate what the user will ask next. The AI should not only be able to suggest other articles that the user has previously read, but also be able to answer questions that the user may have.

Creating the Task

For the AI to complete this task, it will need to use past data to predict what the user will do next effectively. Past data would mean that the AI must know at least the first article you picked to suggest the following article you might enjoy. Going off that point, the AI could answer any questions the user may have after selecting an article to read. Asking the AI without context, or an article chosen would not lead to an optimal response by the AI. The following AI that will be used is ChatGPT, but other mainstream LLMs can be used, like Gemini, Claude, or Perplexity.

Task Part 1: Recommending Next Articles

The following demonstrates how the AI will suggest articles based on previous articles that the user had looked up. The AI will provide articles and continue offering new suggestions for articles the user might want to read. This is a continuous cycle of suggestions, so suggestions will continue once the AI is in the cycle.

  • The following prompt should be: I will pick an article, what article might I want to read next after reading the first article I am about to provide you?
  • Select an article and have the AI produce a list of articles as a suggestion. If the AI only gives one response, tell the AI to make a list consistently.
  • Continue to make a selection to allow the AI to build off the cycle and make more suggestions.
  • This should be able to be used for any article that a user suggests, and if the user wants to move from one topic to another, the AI will be able to adapt to that change and suggest new stories.
